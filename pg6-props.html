PROPS

The show about ad men and women in the 1960s is revered for its taut drama, a slow-burning meditation on life in a bygone era. One of the reasons it's so lauded is because creator Matthew Weiner pays a huge amount of attention to every little onscreen detail. Every inch of the series replicates the '60s as much as humanly possible. That means Weiner's team analyzes every little thing onscreen, Amy Wells, the brilliant set decorator for the iconic television series "Mad Men," played a pivotal role in bringing the show's 1960s-era world to life. With meticulous attention to detail and a keen eye for period authenticity, Wells transformed every set into a visual time capsule, transporting viewers back to the golden age of advertising. Her work not only recreated the look and feel of the era but also subtly reflected the evolving dynamics of the show's characters, infusing each space with character-driven nuance. From the sleek, mid-century modern offices of Sterling Cooper to the stylish homes of the characters, Amy Wells' set decoration added depth and authenticity to "Mad Men," making it a visual masterpiece that remains a benchmark in television history.
