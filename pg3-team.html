Team Members

The cast of "Mad Men" was an incredibly talented and interesting group of actors and characters who played a crucial role in making the show a critical and commercial success. While the series was primarily centered around the enigmatic character of Don Draper, portrayed by Jon Hamm, the supporting cast added depth and complexity to the world of Sterling Cooper, a Madison Avenue advertising agency in the 1960s.

  1. Donald Francis "Don" Draper is a founding partner and the Creative Director at Sterling Cooper Draper Pryce Advertising Agency in Manhattan, NY ("Shut the Door. Have a Seat"). Prior to that position, he was the Director of the Creative Department at the Sterling Cooper Advertising Agency ("Smoke Gets in Your Eyes"). He is regarded among his colleagues as the best to ever pitch copy. His true name is Richard "Dick" Whitman. He stole the identity of a dead officer during the Korean War When we first met Don, the protagonist of the acclaimed television series Mad Men, we met a man who had it all. He was at the pinnacle of his career, happily married to his gorgeous wife, Betty, father of two adorable children, and living in a big house in one of the fanciest suburbs of Westchester. His haughty, arrogant, and aloof facade was easily mistaken for genuine confidence. We soon found out, however, that Don was a man with many flaws. An alcoholic, a womanizer, and an adulterer, he lied about things, not the least of which was his identity. These flaws, or “symptoms” as I think of them, were an indication that something in Don was unwell. Symptoms are often brilliant clues that let an individual know they have underlying yet blocked emotions, often from the past, that need attention and release.

TRIVIA

The actors do not smoke real cigarettes. They smoke Ecstacy herbal cigarettes, which are tobacco and nicotine free. Show creator Matthew Weiner said in a New York Times article, "You don't want actors smoking real cigarettes. They get agitated and nervous. I've been on sets where people throw up, they've smoked so much." When asked what it's like to smoke herbal cigarettes, Jon Hamm (who plays Don Draper) told Vulture, "Terrible.They taste like a mixture between pot and soap."

In 2002, Weiner sent the Mad Men pilot to David Chase, creator of The Sopranos, as a writing sample. In 2012, The New York Times asked Chase how Weiner came to his attention. “We were looking for writers, as we always were, and he was submitted,” Chase recalled. “He told me later that he insisted that he be submitted—his agents didn’t want to do it. And what was submitted to me was the pilot for Mad Men. And it was quite good, and I met with him and he was hired. And then two or three years later, he took that pilot and apparently got somewhere with it.”

Lane Pryce is a distinguished and influential figure in the world of corporate finance and business management. With a rich background in financial expertise and strategic leadership, Lane is a cornerstone of Sterling Cooper & Partners' success. As the Chief Financial Officer and a Partner, his career trajectory is a testament to his unwavering commitment to the company's growth and stability. Lane possesses a strategic vision that extends beyond the numbers. He plays a pivotal role in charting the agency's course for growth and expansion, while simultaneously ensuring fiscal responsibility. Joining Sterling Cooper: Lane Pryce begins his journey with Sterling Cooper as an outsider, having been brought in from the British parent company, PPL. Initially, he faces resistance from some employees, but his financial acumen quickly wins their respect. Promotion to CFO: Lane's competence and contributions lead to his promotion to the role of Chief Financial Officer. In this capacity, he oversees the agency's finances, budgeting, and financial strategy. Becoming a Partner: Lane Pryce's impact on the agency is further solidified when he is made a Partner. His partnership status reflects his integral role in the agency's leadership and decision-making. Mergers and Acquisitions: Lane's expertise proves invaluable during the agency's merger with Puttnam, Powell, and Lowe (PPL). He plays a significant part in navigating this complex merger.
